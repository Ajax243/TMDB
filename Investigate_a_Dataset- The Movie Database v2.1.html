Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

This data set contains information about 10,000 movies collected from The Movie Database (TMDb), including user ratings and revenue.


id : unique id of the movie in the dataset- provides no information (propably will be dropped)
imdb_id: imdb id (probably will be dropped
popularity: a quantifiable measure of popularity on a scale that goes from zero to around 32 for the highest value
budget: reported budget of the movie
revenue : reported revenue of the movie including merchandise and copywrites associated with the movie
original_title: the title of the movie
cast: cast names of the first 5 actors in the cast separated by '|'. Will probably need cleaning
homepage: a website link for the movies, most of them don't have it (probably will be dropped)
director: the name of the director, some small number have more than 1 director separated by '|'
tagline: a catchy line that holds the essense of the movie ( probably will be dropped)
overview: a short description of the movie ( probably will be dropped)
keywords: keywords associated with the movie ( probably will be dropped)
runtime: the length of the movie in minutes
genres</b>: the generes the movie falls under
production_companies: the name of the production company financing the movie
release_date: the date the movie was first premiered
vote_count: the count by which the average vote was calculated
vote_average: the average vote
release_year: release year (int)
budget_adj: budget adjusted for inflation
revenue_adj: revenue adjusted for inflation

Conclusions¶

Regarding Question 1:

  • The top performing actors have starred in movies over 10 times the average
  • The top performing directors have directed movies higher than 5 to 12 times the average directors
  • The top 20 actors by revenue are not the same as top 20 by number of movies
  • The top 20 by adjusted revenue includes actors from the 70s and 80s which were not included in the original list, along with their order, making Harrison Ford is the most successful actor of all time
  • Steven Spielberg's movies have higher than double the second in the all-time list which is James Cameron

Regarding Question 2:

  • Most movies have revenues less than 50 Million USD
  • For a film maker, the best time to release your movie is June, followed by December or May
  • For a film maker, the least profitable time to release your movie is September, August and February respectively

Regarding Question 3:

  • Highly rated movies are not necessarily popular movies and vice versa
  • Popularity positively affects the revenue of the movie
  • For a film maker, higher budget movies are correlated with higher revenues

Limitations:

  • Movies with missing values for revenues, budgets or actors have been dropped from the dataset which must have affected the statistics computed
  • Actor revenues aggregates the sum of all the movies he appeared in which is not accurate
  • Actor revenues is only aggregated if their names were one of the first three names
  • Actor Movie count is only calculated if their names were one of the first three names
  • Movies with a cast like Avengers and Starwars where there are a lot of main characters have not been accredited to the full main cast

Further Work:

  • Average vote should have a minimum vote count to avoid outliers
  • The combination of Actor/Director along with the revenue could be insightful
  • Actors and directors could be divided into categories each having their own statistics
  • Movies could be aggregated based on Genres and Production companies with an analysis of revenue/ budget per genre/production company, which would be insightful
  • If the revenue could be divided into US and Abroad, will generate a completely different outlook
